## Key Research Findings

### Chest Exercises
- Cable crossovers maintain constant tension superior to free weights
- Pec deck activated pectoralis major at 98% of bench press levels (Welsch et al. 2005)
- Pulley height significantly affects muscle fiber recruitment

### Hamstring Exercises
- Hip lifts show varying glute:hamstring ratios by position
  - Unilateral bridge: 111.3% ratio
  - Hip extension: 59.2% ratio
- Deadlift: erector spinae and quadriceps > gluteus maximus and biceps femoris
- Leg curls produce 48.78% MVIC (moderate intensity)
- Lying variations preferentially activate biceps femoris
- Most exercises show 6-26% higher semitendinosus vs biceps femoris activation

### Quadriceps Exercises
- Barbell squats: peak vastus lateralis activation at 90° knee flexion
- Lunges produce >60% MVIC gluteus maximus activation (very high)
- Step-ups show highest vastus lateralis and medialis EMG activity
- Hack squats significantly reduce erector spinae and hamstring activation
- Diagonal lunges enhance frontal plane stability critical for sports performance
