## BENT-OVER BARBELL ROW VARIATIONS

### 1. Bent-Over Barbell Row (Standard Pronated Grip)

**Primary Muscles Worked:**
- Latissimus Dorsi (high activation)
- Rhomboids Major and Minor (high activation with pronated grip)
- Middle Trapezius (moderate-high activation)
- Lower Trapezius (moderate activation)
- Posterior Deltoid (higher activation vs supinated grip)

**Secondary Muscles:**
- Erector Spinae (anti-flexion stabilization - highest lumbar load of all row variations)
- Biceps Brachii (moderate activation, less than supinated grip)
- Brachialis
- Brachioradialis
- Core musculature (rectus abdominis, external/internal obliques)
- Hamstrings (isometric hip extensor stabilization)

**Scientific Reference:**
Fenwick, C. M. J., Brown, S. H. M., & McGill, S. M. (2009). Comparison of different rowing exercises: trunk muscle activation and lumbar spine motion, load, and stiffness. *Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research*, 23(2), 350-358. DOI: 10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181942019

**Sports Tags:**
- Rowing/Crew
- American Football (defensive linemen, linebackers)
- Wrestling/Grappling sports
- CrossFit
- Rugby

**Key Biomechanical Notes:**
- Pronated grip emphasizes rhomboids and posterior deltoids more than supinated variations
- Produces highest lumbar spine compressive and shear forces among row variations
- Requires significant erector spinae activation for torso stabilization

- Rock Climbing

- Swimming

- Similar lumbar loading to standard bent-over row

## SUMMARY OF KEY EMG RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Grip Width Effects (Pull-Up/Lat Pulldown):
- **Wide Grip:** Emphasizes latissimus dorsi width (upper/outer fibers), increased teres major and infraspinatus activation
- **Narrow Grip:** Increases biceps brachii involvement, emphasizes lower lat fibers
- **Note:** Overall lat activation similar across grip widths with pronated grip, but fiber emphasis shifts

### Grip Orientation Effects (Rowing):
- **Pronated (Overhand):** Higher rhomboid, middle trapezius, and posterior deltoid activation; lower biceps involvement
- **Supinated (Underhand):** Significantly higher biceps brachii activation; emphasis on lower lats
- **Neutral (Hammer):** Balanced activation; increased brachialis and brachioradialis; joint-friendly

### Unilateral vs Bilateral Rowing:
- **Unilateral/Alternating:** Significantly higher external oblique activation (anti-rotation); corrects imbalances
- **Bilateral:** Higher erector spinae and multifidus activation; allows heavier total loads

### Spinal Loading Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest):
1. Bent-Over Barbell Row (unsupported)
2. Bent-Over Dumbbell Row (unsupported)
3. Landmine/T-Bar Row
4. Seated Cable/Machine Row
5. Chest-Supported Row (minimal spinal load)

### Core Activation Patterns:
- **Anti-Rotation:** Unilateral rows, alternating rows, single-arm cable rows (external obliques primary)
- **Anti-Flexion:** Bilateral bent-over rows (erector spinae, multifidus primary)
- **Minimal Core:** Chest-supported rows, machine rows with chest pad
