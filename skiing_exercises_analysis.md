## Research Summary

### Biomechanical Demands of Alpine Skiing

Alpine skiing requires:
- **Eccentric quadriceps strength** (controlling descent, absorbing impacts)
- **Gluteal strength and hip stability** (lateral movements, balance)
- **Core anti-rotation strength** (maintaining posture during turns)
- **Hamstring strength** (knee stabilization, ACL protection)
- **Lower back/erector spinae endurance** (maintaining athletic position)
- **Adductor/abductor strength** (edge control, lateral stability)
- **Calf strength** (ankle stability, edge control)
- **Upper body pulling strength** (pole plant, recovery from falls)
- **Plyometric power** (moguls, jumps, rapid direction changes)

### Key Research Findings

1. **Eccentric Strength Critical**: Recreational skiing causes prolonged eccentric quadriceps and hamstring fatigue. Concentric exercises (biking, walking) do NOT prevent skiing-specific fatigue.

2. **Hamstring Activation**: About 55% of downhill ski injuries involve lower extremities. Hamstring activation reduces ACL strain during jump landings.

3. **Hip Strength = Knee Protection**: Lower preseason hip abduction and external rotation strength increases ACL injury risk.

4. **Core Anti-Rotation**: Trunk must maintain fall line position while lower body rotates side-to-side for turns. Requires anti-rotation and stabilization strength.

5. **Nordic Hamstring Exercise**: Reduces hamstring injury rates by up to 51% through eccentric conditioning.

1. **Nordic Hamstring Curl** - 51% injury reduction
2. **Bulgarian Split Squat** - Single-leg eccentric strength
3. **Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** - Eccentric hamstring/glute
4. **Pallof Press** - Anti-rotation core
5. **Box Jumps** - Plyometric power
6. **Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift** - Balance + posterior chain
7. **Lateral Lunge variations** - Edge control
8. **Front/Side Plank** - Core stability foundation

### Priority Tier 2 (HIGH VALUE)
1. **Goblet Squat** - Beginner-friendly quad eccentric
2. **Dead Bug / Bird Dog** - Dynamic core stability
3. **Step-Ups** - Single-leg quad strength
4. **Glute-Ham Raise** - Advanced hamstring eccentric
5. **Cable Chop/Woodchop** - Dynamic anti-rotation
6. **Single-Leg Box Squat** - Advanced eccentric control
7. **Squat Jumps** - Plyometric quad power
8. **Pull-Ups** - Upper body pulling strength

### Priority Tier 3 (SUPPLEMENTARY)
1. All other squat variations
2. Additional calf raise variations
3. Olympic lift variations (for advanced athletes)
4. Additional core variations
5. Rowing variations

## Training Program Structure Recommendations

### Phase 1: Base Building (8-12 weeks pre-season)
**Focus:** Eccentric strength, single-leg stability, core foundation
- 3-4x/week strength training
- 2x/week plyometrics (low volume)
- Emphasize: Bulgarian split squat, RDL, Nordic curls, planks

### Phase 2: Power Development (4-6 weeks pre-season)
**Focus:** Plyometric power, reactive strength, sport-specific movements
- 2-3x/week strength maintenance
- 2-3x/week plyometrics (moderate volume)
- Emphasize: Box jumps, squat jumps, lateral movements, anti-rotation

### Phase 3: Peak/Maintenance (during ski season)
**Focus:** Maintain strength, prevent injury, recover between ski days
- 2x/week strength maintenance
- 1x/week plyometrics (low volume)
- Emphasize: Nordic curls, single-leg work, core stability

### Weekly Template Example (Base Building Phase)

**Day 1: Lower Body Eccentric + Core**
- Bulgarian Split Squat: 3×8/leg
- Romanian Deadlift: 3×10
- Nordic Hamstring Curl: 3×6
- Pallof Press: 3×12/side
- Dead Bug: 3×10/side

**Day 2: Upper Body + Plyometrics**
- Pull-Ups: 4×6-8
- Bent-Over Row: 3×10
- Box Jumps: 4×5

## Conclusion

This analysis identified **156 exercises** from the CoachWhitt database v3.1 that are scientifically validated or biomechanically relevant for skiing performance and injury prevention. The exercises are organized by primary biomechanical benefit and supported by peer-reviewed research and professional ski conditioning programs.

**Key Priorities:**
1. **Eccentric strength training** (Nordic curls, RDLs, Bulgarian split squats)
2. **Single-leg stability** (all single-leg variations)
3. **Anti-rotation core** (Pallof press, cable chops, planks)
4. **Plyometric power** (box jumps, squat jumps)
5. **Lateral movement** (lateral lunges, lateral step-ups)

These exercises should be integrated into a periodized training program starting 8-12 weeks before ski season, with emphasis shifting from base building → power development → in-season maintenance.
