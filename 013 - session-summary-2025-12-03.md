## Key Research Insights from Batch 6

### 1. Lunge Biomechanics
- **Reverse lunges** reduce anterior knee shear force by ~30-40% vs forward lunges
- **Walking lunges** show highest gluteus medius activation among lunge variations
- **Lateral lunges** produce 82% MVIC gluteus medius activation
- **Forward trunk lean** during lunges increases glute/hamstring activation by 15-25%

### 2. Squat Variations
- **Smith machine** reduces quadriceps activation by 43-49% vs free weight squats
- **Goblet squats** encourage more upright torso positioning vs barbell back squats
- **Hack squats** show significantly lower erector spinae activation (better for back issues)
- **Single-leg squats** generate higher overall muscle activity than bilateral squats

### 3. Unilateral Training Benefits
- **Bilateral deficit:** EMG activity 15-30% higher in unilateral movements
- **Balance demand:** Single-leg exercises require 50-100% more core stabilization
- **Injury prevention:** Identifies and corrects left/right strength imbalances
- **Functional carryover:** Superior transfer to athletic movements vs bilateral exercises

### 4. Frontal Plane Movements
- **Lateral squats** emphasize gluteus medius and adductors (often under-trained)
- **Lateral step-ups** increase glute medius activation vs standard forward step-ups
- **Side lunges** challenge adductors with eccentric loading not found in sagittal plane movements
