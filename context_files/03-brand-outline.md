# CoachWhitt Brand Outline

This document defines the core identity, purpose, and positioning of the CoachWhitt brand. For visual specifications (colors, logos, typography), refer to `04-visual-brand-guide.md`.

---

## 1. Company Purpose

**Core Purpose:** To help people improve in their sporting and professional needs through coaching.

**Business Model:**
- Start with online personal training for scalability (leveraging qualifications in gym instruction, personal training, and nutrition)
- Charge premium for face-to-face interactions
- Long-term expansion into elite sports coaching (online/face-to-face) and public speaking

**Key Credentials:**
- Elite American Flag Football coach (Team GB Women)
- European Silver Medal 2019
- European Gold Medal 2023
- World #4 ranking 2023

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary Segments:**

### Beginners
- Individuals who can benefit from passive/automated guidance (documents/videos)
- Need structure and foundational knowledge
- Looking for approachable, non-intimidating coaching

### Experienced Athletes
- Seeking added value through nutrition and elite coaching expertise
- Want to level up performance
- Appreciate technical precision and candid feedback

### Career Professionals & Parents
- Age range: Generally 20-60 (athletes 18-30)
- Need to lose weight/get fitter but have lifestyle commitments
- Require solutions that fit around work and family life
- Relate to founder's experience as parent of two young children

## 6. Brand Story

### The 2023 European Championship Final (Limerick, Ireland)

**Setup:**
Facing Spain — the same team that defeated us in the 2019 final. Four years of waiting, complicated by COVID shutdowns and internal governing body chaos. The team trained through lockdowns, uncertainty, and organizational turmoil.

**The Challenge:**
Despite a strong offensive game plan, Spain's key player was unstoppable. The defense struggled. Emotions ran high on the sideline. Halftime arrived with everything on the line.

**The Turning Point:**
At halftime, calm leadership prevailed. Instead of panic, strategic adjustment. The defensive captain was pulled aside. One small tweak to play-calling. Calm delivery. Confidence reinforced.

**The Result:**
The composure trickled down to the team. Second half: crucial defensive stops. Momentum shifted. Final play of the game — defense held. Final score: 26-19. European Champions.

**The Lesson:**
This story embodies the CoachWhitt approach:
- Calmness under pressure
- Strategic thinking over emotional reaction
- Small adjustments, not wholesale changes
- Leadership by example
- Preparation meeting opportunity

## 8. Tagline

### Primary Tagline
**"Train Like an Athlete. Live Like You."**

This tagline captures the brand's unique position:
- **"Train Like an Athlete"** → Elite-level coaching methods and mindset
- **"Live Like You"** → Practical application for real-world life

### Formatting Guideline
When space allows, display the tagline on two lines for optimal visual impact:
```
Train Like an Athlete.
Live Like You.
```

This two-line format:
- Creates better visual hierarchy
- Makes the personalized "Live Like You" message stand out
- Improves readability and brand recognition
- Should be used in headers, footers, and prominent placements

**Note:** In constrained spaces (social media bios, small badges, etc.), single-line usage is acceptable.

### Alternative Slogans (for reference)
- "Elite Performance – Everyday Balance."
- "Discipline Meets Balance — Train Like an Athlete, Anywhere."
- "Your Coach Has Medals — Now It's Your Turn to Win Yours."
- "Premium personal coaching crafted by Team GB experience — tailored to your life."
